Who is the Best Big Man in the NBA?

2022-03-31

There are so many great big men in the NBA right now, but who is the best?

Because of the recent emphasis on spacing and 3-point shooting, the role of the big man has diminished in recent years. Teams now opt for fewer “non-shooters” on the court at a time, and as a result lineups have gotten smaller. Big men in the NBA might be smaller in number today, but their impact isn’t any less.

Players such as Giannis Antetokounmpo, Joel Embiid, Nikola Jokić, Kevin Durant, and Karl Anthony-Towns are all leading their respective playoff-bound teams and are atop the MVP conversation. Rudy Gobert and Bam Adebayo are defensive stalwarts who are valuable to their team’s success without the high-end offense.

These players among others are all important to winning, but recent data from Basketball Reference will help determine who has the biggest overall impact.

Talk about a photo finish. So besides basic individual stats, what else illustrates how well a player performed?

Advanced stats of course!

We’ll put these individual statistics in context along with new metrics like “PER” and “VORP” to see who stands out from the rest. PER stands for player efficiency rating, which is a measure of a player’s per-minute productivity. VORP stands for value over replacement player which estimates the points per 100 possessions that a player contributed above a replacement-level player. These statistics show how efficient a player is, and also how much better they perform compared to an average big man.

Nikola Jokić is on pace to beat Giannis’s currently held record for the highest PER rating in a single season. On top of that, he leads the other bigs in VORP by a significant margin. This is because Jokić is the only one of these great players who doesn’t have an all-star teammate playing alongside him. He is forced more than any other player to create plays for his team and he constantly delivers.

However, we’re not ready to crown Jokić as the king of big men quite yet. Even though he is the most efficient and “valuable” player according to our data, does it lead to winning?

“Win Shares” are an advanced measure that are given to players based on their offense, defense, and playing time. One win share is worth one-third of a team win. If a team were to win 60 games, there would be 180 win shares to distribute among that team’s players.

Despite having a worse record than some of the other players, it’s not bad enough to really hurt Jokić. In fact, he leads the league in win shares. Giannis and the Milwaukee Bucks might have a better record than Nikola and the Denver Nuggets, but Jokić as an individual leads to more winning.

Earlier it was difficult to determine who the best big man was because of how similar their seasons looked, but after diving into advanced stats we found that Nikola Jokić stands apart from the rest. Not only is he the best big man, but the best overall player in the league. Despite the decreasing numbers of power-forwards and centers in today’s NBA, the value and importance of those players is at an all-time high.
